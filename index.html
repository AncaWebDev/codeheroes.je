Coding for kids is like learning a new language. We use code to communicate with computers and build apps, websites, games, robots and much more!

Why should kids learn to code?

Skills for the digital age


Coding develops strong problem-solving skills, teaching kids how to solve anything in a logical and creative way and improves their attention to detail. Group projects also develop their team building and communication skills which will help them succeed in the social and academic environments of the future.
Coding sets them up for a challenge where they learn that failing is normal and that if they keep trying anything is possible. It also teaches them that multiple solutions can exist for the same problem thereby building up their resilience and encouraging persistence.

Set them up for a successful future


As more and more aspects of our lives are becoming automated, the demand for programmers and digital-literate people is rapidly increasing. According to this journal, currently half of the highest-paying jobs in America require applicants to have some sort of computer knowledge or skill. Knowing how to code will probably become the new standard of basic literacy(the new knowing how to read and write).

Make learning fun


Coding is a rewarding activity that makes kids want to learn more as they discover the joy of building new things from scratch and the satisfaction of solving puzzles. With code, kids can turn any idea into reality as well as program drones, robots, cars and more, making the process of learning so much fun!

Code Heroes combines the best teaching tools and methods to keep the kids engaged and curious.

Scratch.mit


Scratch is a visual programming language developed by MIT that allows students to build interactive animations, games and stories. Scratch has a community of people from all over the world, where everyone can collaborate on projects and share their work with others.

Creative Computing Curriculum


A creative curriculum created by Harvard Graduate School of Education comprised of a series of ideas, strategies and activities for an introduction to Scratch programming language. It suggests some popular games and activities suitable for beginners and introduces key programming concepts through play.
